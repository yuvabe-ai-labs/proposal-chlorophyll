# The Agentic Spectrum — Vision, and What This Engagement Delivers

**Document purpose:** Maps the agentic spectrum onto chlorophyll's engagement, and is explicit about
the line between the **long-term vision** (the full ladder) and **what the 12–16-week engagement
actually commits to**. The five **phases** are the top-level structure; under each, the **capability
level(s)** it touches are broken into three building blocks — **Skill**, **Process**, and
**Knowledge** — and tagged for scope.

> **Read this as an indicative shape, not a fixed plan.** We haven't run Discovery yet, so the
> chlorophyll-specific details below — which practices get codified, and what data exists to draw on
> — are illustrative of the *kind* of thing each level needs, not a commitment to a known structure.
> Discovery is what establishes the real shape of the method and the data.

---

## The three building blocks

Every rung is built from the same three things — and it helps to be precise about what each one is:

- **Skill** — *the AI skill that has to be built.* The new capability the system gains at this rung:
  retrieve, draft, reason, critique, orchestrate, operate.
- **Process** — *which chlorophyll way of working gets codified here.* Not the system's plumbing —
  the actual human practice we capture and encode: how strategists recall, draft, synthesize,
  challenge, run a workshop, deliver.
- **Knowledge** — *what data exists, or might be needed, that this rung can draw on.* The material: past
  decks, frameworks, decision rationale, market context. Some of it plainly exists; much of it is
  tacit or has to be created — which is exactly what Discovery is for.

## How to read this

- **It's cumulative.** Each level inherits everything below it and adds.
- **Knowledge is the gating dependency.** The higher rungs need data that doesn't neatly exist yet —
  *how* seniors decide, *why* options were killed, the strategist archetypes. Discovery is what
  reveals which data exists, which is tacit, and which must be created — which is why the top rungs
  are **Shaped by Discovery**.
- **Vision vs. scope.** The full ladder (Recall → Operate) is the *destination*. This engagement
  commits only to the lower rungs; the higher ones are marked **Shaped by Discovery** throughout.

---

## What this engagement delivers — and what comes later

**In scope — this 12–16-week engagement:** the Discovery foundation → **Recall** → **Assist** →
**early Reason**, plus the **external-research layer** (as data, not yet a critique agent) and, at
the end, an **integrated, human-gated workflow**. In short: a genuinely useful internal Strategy
Brain that recalls, drafts, and assists reasoning — with the strategist in control throughout.

**Shaped by Discovery — scoped and priced once Discovery confirms their value:** full autonomous
**Reason**, **Critique** (the sparring partner), **Orchestrate** (the multi-agent workshop), and
**Operate** (the autonomous pipeline). These need data and method that Discovery has to surface
first — real and reachable, but not committed inside this engagement.

**Scope key:** ✅ in scope · ◐ in scope, early form · ○ shaped by Discovery

## Phase 1 — Discovery & Audit · *2–3 weeks* · ✅ in scope
**The foundation beneath every level** *(pre-Recall)*

This phase builds no capability level — it surfaces the **Process** and the **Knowledge** that every
level above depends on: mapping how strategy actually moves from client input to brand core,
territory and options; finding where the proprietary frameworks and internal methods enter the work;
**capturing how strategists decide, not just what they ship**; and auditing what data exists, what's
tacit, and what's reusable vs. confidential. Discovery is what turns the indicative shapes below into
a concrete, scoped build.

---

## Phase 2 — Focused Strategy Brain Prototype · *2–3 weeks*
**Delivers:** Recall, and a focused Assist on one high-value workflow.

### Level 1 — Recall · ✅ in scope
*"Surface the closest past cases, patterns, and frameworks for this brief."*

- **Skill (the AI skill to build):** semantic search & retrieval; brief-to-case similarity matching;
  summarization.
- **Process (chlorophyll practice codified):** how strategists recall and reuse past work — which
  precedents they reach for, and why.
- **Knowledge (data that exists / might be needed):** the case library — past brand-core, territory,
  strategy and naming decks, and project archives. *Exists,* but scattered and lightly tagged; *might
  need* consistent metadata to be usable.

### Level 2 — Assist · ✅ in scope *(focused to one workflow in this phase)*
*"Draft a first-cut territory / workshop question set / brief summary."*

- **Skill:** generative drafting; tone-and-voice conditioning; brief comprehension.
- **Process:** the briefing & first-draft routine — how a brief is interpreted and turned into a
  first-cut territory or workshop set.
- **Knowledge:** output templates, the house voice, and gold-standard exemplars. *Exists* inside past
  decks; *might need* to be distilled into reusable templates.

## Phase 3 — Internal Strategy Brain · *3–4 weeks*
**Delivers:** a fuller internal knowledge layer and *assisted* reasoning — structured option support
and pattern surfacing, with the strategist driving. Full autonomous reasoning is Shaped by Discovery.

### Level 3 — Reason · ◐ in scope, early form
*"Brief → category map → territory options → pressure-test against the frameworks → structured
option set."*

> **Scope note:** in this engagement, Reason arrives in *assisted* form — the system structures,
> surfaces, and supports; the strategist drives the chain. The fully autonomous version is a later
> step, Shaped by Discovery.

- **Skill:** multi-step chaining; framework application; divergent option generation; convergent
  synthesis.
- **Process:** chlorophyll's core strategy method — moving from brief to brand core, territory and
  options, and how the proprietary frameworks (anthrop™️, wholon™️, ideantity™️, litmosi™️) and the
  internal strategy algorithms are applied.
- **Knowledge:** the frameworks and algorithms themselves, decision heuristics (strong vs. weak), and
  a pattern library of past strategic moves. *Lives tacitly* in senior heads and decks; *might need*
  to be elicited and represented — a core Discovery question.

---

## Phase 4 — External Research Layer · *3–4 weeks*
**Delivers (in scope):** the external research layer — market, category, competitor and cultural
data folded into the Knowledge. **Shaped by Discovery:** Critique — the research layer is what later
gives a critique agent something credible to test against.

### Level 4 — Critique · ○ shaped by Discovery
*"Stress-test the direction — where does this positioning break? What's the contrarian read?"*

> **Scope note:** this is what the Phase-4 research data *enables next*, not a Phase-4 deliverable.
> A sparring-partner agent is a later, separately-scoped capability.

- **Skill:** adversarial red-teaming; rubric-based evaluation; consistency checking; confidence
  calibration.
- **Process:** the senior challenge — the decision points where strategists interrogate,
  pressure-test, and choose between options.
- **Knowledge:** quality signals (what strong vs. derivative looks like), the rationale behind past
  *rejections*, and external market/competitor reality. *Mostly might-exist* — rejections are rarely
  recorded, and market data sits outside the firm.

---

## Phase 5 — Integration · *2 weeks*
**Delivers (in scope):** the internal Brain and the external layer composed into one **integrated,
human-gated workflow** with a review/approval flow — the first, governed step toward autonomous
operation. **Shaped by Discovery:** Orchestrate and Operate.

### Level 5 — Orchestrate · ○ shaped by Discovery
*"Run a synthetic workshop — role-agents (category analyst, semiotician, consumer-truth finder,
contrarian, naming agent) generate and debate options."*

> **Scope note:** the multi-agent workshop is a major, separately-scoped build — a horizon the
> integrated workflow points toward, not something Phase 5 ships in two weeks.

- **Skill:** multi-agent orchestration; role specialization; debate and arbitration.
- **Process:** the strategy workshop flow — how a real chlorophyll session moves from founder/client
  inputs, through multiple perspectives, to a shared direction.
- **Knowledge:** strategist role/persona archetypes, workshop scripts and question templates, and the
  cross-domain reference each role draws on. *Some exists* (templates); *much might-exist* (archetypes
  to be defined in Discovery).

### Level 6 — Operate · ○ shaped by Discovery
*"Brief-to-territory pipeline runs end-to-end autonomously; strategist approves at defined gates."*

> **Scope note:** the integrated workflow from Phase 5 is the *human-gated* first step; fully
> autonomous, event-driven operation is a later horizon, scoped once trust and quality are proven.

- **Skill:** end-to-end execution; autonomous triggering; quality-gating; continuous learning.
- **Process:** the end-to-end delivery & sign-off process — how work moves from brief to client-ready
  output through review and approval gates.
- **Knowledge:** live operational data (incoming briefs, new cases, outcomes), governance/permission
  policy, and quality thresholds. *Mostly might-exist* — created as the system runs.

## How each block evolves across the levels

Each block has a clear centre-of-gravity shift as the system climbs.

- **Skill (what the AI does):** retrieve → draft → reason → critique → orchestrate → operate. *From
  passive to active to autonomous.*
- **Process (chlorophyll practice codified):** case recall → the first-draft routine → the core
  synthesis method → the decision points → the workshop flow → the whole delivery process. *It moves
  from codifying a simple reuse habit to codifying the firm's entire way of working.*
- **Knowledge (data drawn on):** data that plainly exists (past decks) → data that exists but is
  tacit (how seniors decide) → data that must be created (recorded rejections, archetypes, live
  outcomes). *The higher you climb, the more the data has to be elicited, not just collected.*

## Why this matters for how the system is built

1. **Knowledge is the gating dependency — and it's what Discovery uncovers.** The higher rungs need
   data that doesn't tidily exist yet (how seniors decide, why options were killed, role archetypes).
   Discovery determines what exists, what's tacit, and what must be created — which is precisely why
   Critique, Orchestrate and Operate are *Shaped by Discovery*.
2. **You're codifying the firm, not buying features.** Each rung captures more of chlorophyll's *way
   of working* (Process) and more of its *material* (Knowledge), powered by a stacking set of AI
   capabilities (Skill). These are durable, compounding assets — your method becomes software you
   own, and the same engine later extends into marketing and creative.
3. **The lower rungs are the commitment; the higher rungs are the trajectory.** This engagement
   delivers Recall → Assist → early Reason on a solid foundation. Everything above is real, reachable,
   and unlocked by that foundation — but scoped and priced only once Discovery proves the data and
   the method are there.
