# The Agentic Spectrum — Vision, and What This Engagement Delivers

**Document purpose:** Maps the agentic spectrum onto chlorophyll's engagement, and is explicit about
the line between the **long-term vision** (the full ladder) and **what the 12–16-week engagement
actually commits to**. The five **phases** are the top-level structure; under each, the **capability
level(s)** it touches are broken into **Skill**, **Process**, and **Knowledge Base** — and tagged
for scope.

> **Read this as an indicative shape, not a fixed plan.** We haven't run Discovery yet, so the
> chlorophyll-specific details below — especially how the proprietary frameworks and internal
> methods are represented — are illustrative of the *kind* of thing each level needs, not a
> commitment to a known structure. Discovery is what establishes the real shape of the data and the
> method.

---

## The three building blocks

- **Skill** — what the AI/agent can *do*: discrete, reusable capabilities (retrieve, draft, apply a
  framework, critique, orchestrate). The tools in the agent's hands.
- **Process** — how the work *flows*: the orchestration, sequencing, checkpoints, and — most
  importantly — the **human gates**. The workflow, and who approves what.
- **Knowledge Base** — what the system *knows*: the grounding it reasons over (cases, frameworks,
  decision rationale, taxonomies, external context). The substrate.

## How to read this

- **It's cumulative.** Each level inherits everything below it and adds.
- **The Knowledge Base is the gating dependency.** You literally cannot build Reason, Critique, or
  Orchestrate until the KB encodes chlorophyll's *method* — not just its documents. This is why
  Discovery (which harvests and structures the KB) is the foundation everything else stands on.
- **Vision vs. scope.** The full ladder (Recall → Operate) is the *destination*. This engagement
  commits only to the lower rungs; the higher ones are *future horizons*, marked throughout.

---

## What this engagement delivers — and what comes later

**In scope — this 12–16-week engagement:** the Discovery foundation → **Recall** → **Assist** →
**early Reason**, plus the **external-research layer** (as knowledge, not yet a critique agent) and,
at the end, an **integrated, human-gated workflow**. In short: a genuinely useful internal Strategy
Brain that recalls, drafts, and assists reasoning — with the strategist in control throughout.

**Future horizons — scoped and priced later** (consistent with the proposal's *"later phases can be
priced and scoped based on validated value"* and its **future-expansion** section): full autonomous
**Reason**, **Critique** (the sparring partner), **Orchestrate** (the multi-agent workshop), and
**Operate** (the autonomous pipeline). The foundation we build is exactly what *unlocks* these — but
we don't promise them inside this engagement.

**Scope key:** ✅ in scope · ◐ in scope, early form · ○ future horizon (beyond this engagement)

## Phase 1 — Discovery & Audit · *2–3 weeks* · ✅ in scope
**The foundation beneath every level** *(pre-Recall)*

This phase builds no capability level — it harvests and structures the **Knowledge Base** that every
level above depends on: inventorying the case library, building a shared brand-strategy vocabulary,
mapping where the proprietary frameworks and internal methods enter the work, and **capturing how
strategists decide, not just what they ship**. It also maps the current workflow and marks where
human judgment must stay non-negotiable. Discovery is what turns the indicative shapes below into a
concrete, scoped build.

---

## Phase 2 — Focused Strategy Brain Prototype · *2–3 weeks*
**Delivers:** Recall, and a focused Assist on one high-value workflow.

### Level 1 — Recall · ✅ in scope
*"Surface the closest past cases, patterns, and frameworks for this brief."*

- **Skill:** semantic search & retrieval; brief-to-case similarity matching; summarization; source
  citation back to the original deck.
- **Process:** single-step (query → ranked results); strategist initiates, zero autonomy;
  thumbs-up/down relevance feedback; a permission/confidentiality check on every result.
- **Knowledge Base:** digitized case library (brand core, territory, strategy, naming decks); rich
  metadata/tagging (sector, challenge, audience, deliverable, outcome); a brand-strategy taxonomy
  (what *is* a territory vs. a core); a vector index; the proprietary frameworks documented
  descriptively.

### Level 2 — Assist · ✅ in scope *(focused to one workflow in this phase)*
*"Draft a first-cut territory / workshop question set / brief summary."*

- **Skill:** generative drafting; structured/template-filling generation; tone-and-voice
  conditioning (write in chlorophyll's idiom); brief comprehension (pull intent, audience,
  constraints from messy client input).
- **Process:** single-pass generate → **strategist edits = the human gate**; prompt scaffolding per
  output type; draft versioning; edits captured as a training signal.
- **Knowledge Base:** output templates (territory, core, question banks); a style/voice guide;
  gold-standard exemplars for grounding; a map of which framework feeds which output.

## Phase 3 — Internal Strategy Brain · *3–4 weeks*
**Delivers:** a fuller internal knowledge layer and *assisted* reasoning — structured option support
and pattern surfacing, with the strategist driving. Full autonomous reasoning chains mature beyond
this engagement.

### Level 3 — Reason · ◐ in scope, early form
*"Brief → category map → territory options → pressure-test against the frameworks → structured
option set."*

> **Scope note:** in this engagement, Reason arrives in *assisted* form — the system structures,
> surfaces, and supports; the strategist drives the chain. The fully autonomous version below is the
> direction it grows toward, not a Phase-3 deliverable.

- **Skill:** multi-step planning/chaining (decompose a brief into a reasoning sequence); framework
  application; divergent option generation; convergent synthesis (cluster, compare, rank); tool use
  (calls the retrieval and framework skills).
- **Process:** an orchestrated chain with named intermediate artifacts; **mid-chain checkpoints**
  where the strategist can inspect and redirect; state carried across steps; a full reasoning-path
  trace.
- **Knowledge Base:** chlorophyll's proprietary frameworks (anthrop™️, wholon™️, ideantity™️,
  litmosi™️) **represented as callable reasoning operators** rather than passive documents — the
  exact mapping is what Discovery establishes; the internal strategy procedures formalized into
  explicit steps; decision heuristics (what makes a direction strong vs. weak); a pattern library of
  recurring strategic moves.

## Phase 4 — External Research Layer · *3–4 weeks*
**Delivers (in scope):** the external research layer — market, category, competitor and cultural
intelligence folded into the Knowledge Base. **Sets up (future horizon):** Critique — the research
layer is what later gives a critique agent something credible to test against.

### Level 4 — Critique · ○ future horizon
*"Stress-test the direction — where does this positioning break? What's the contrarian read?"*

> **Scope note:** this is what the Phase-4 research layer *enables next*, not a Phase-4 deliverable.
> A sparring-partner agent is a later, separately-scoped capability.

- **Skill:** adversarial reasoning / red-teaming; rubric-based evaluation; devil's-advocate
  generation; consistency checking (does core → territory → naming → message hold together?);
  confidence calibration (flag what's weak).
- **Process:** an iterative **generate → critique → revise** loop; the agent challenges, **the
  strategist adjudicates (human as judge)**; an explicit critique rubric; critiques and resolutions
  logged back into the KB.
- **Knowledge Base:** quality signals — what "weak / clichéd / derivative" looks like, and past
  strategies that *didn't* land; captured decision rationale (why options were rejected — the real
  craft); a risk/objection library; external market reality to test against.

## Phase 5 — Integration · *2 weeks*
**Delivers (in scope):** the internal Brain and the external layer composed into one **integrated,
human-gated workflow** with a review/approval flow — the first, governed step toward autonomous
operation. **Sets up (future horizons):** Orchestrate and Operate.

### Level 5 — Orchestrate · ○ future horizon
*"Run a synthetic workshop — role-agents (category analyst, semiotician, consumer-truth finder,
contrarian, naming agent) generate and debate options."*

> **Scope note:** the multi-agent workshop is a major, separately-scoped build — a horizon the
> integrated workflow points toward, not something Phase 5 ships in two weeks.

- **Skill:** multi-agent orchestration (spin up, route, coordinate); role specialization (each agent
  a distinct skill profile); debate / negotiation / consensus; meta-reasoning and arbitration (when
  is the debate done?); conflict resolution across contradictory outputs.
- **Process:** an orchestration topology (parallel divergence → structured debate → convergence); a
  director/arbiter pattern; turn-taking protocols; **the human as workshop facilitator at the
  gates**; the debate "transcript" saved as a reusable artifact.
- **Knowledge Base:** role/persona definitions (what each agent knows, values, and how it reasons —
  encoded chlorophyll archetypes); a shared-workspace/blackboard schema; workshop-flow templates
  (how a real chlorophyll session runs); cross-domain knowledge per role (semiotics, culture, naming
  conventions).

### Level 6 — Operate · ○ future horizon
*"Brief-to-territory pipeline runs end-to-end autonomously; strategist approves at defined gates."*

> **Scope note:** the integrated workflow from Phase 5 is the *human-gated* first step; fully
> autonomous, event-driven operation is a later horizon, scoped once trust and quality are proven.

- **Skill:** end-to-end workflow execution; autonomous triggering; self-monitoring / error handling
  / fallback; quality-gating (decide what's auto-pass vs. needs a human); continuous learning from
  outcomes.
- **Process:** event-driven triggers (a brief arrives → it kicks off); **non-negotiable human
  approval gates**; exception/escalation paths; monitoring, logging, audit, rollback; governance and
  compliance enforced *in* the flow.
- **Knowledge Base:** live, continuously-ingested knowledge (new cases + streaming external
  signals); governance policy as data (what's allowed autonomously, escalation rules); encoded
  quality thresholds/SLAs; full provenance and lineage.

## How each block evolves across the levels

The reason this decomposition is useful: each block has a clear centre-of-gravity shift as the
system climbs the *full* ladder — vision and engagement alike.

- **Knowledge Base:** documents → structured & tagged → **encoded method** → **captured judgment** →
  role-knowledge → live & governed. *It moves from "what we made" to "how we think."*
- **Skill:** retrieve → generate → reason → critique → orchestrate → operate. *From passive to
  active to autonomous.*
- **Process:** pull → generate-and-edit → chain-with-checkpoints → critique-loop → multi-agent →
  event-driven-with-gates. *And the strategist's role shifts: operator → editor → director → judge →
  approver — always accountable for the final output.*

## Why this matters for how the system is built

1. **The Knowledge Base is the gating dependency — and it's exactly what Discovery harvests.** You
   can't reach Reason or Orchestrate without encoding the frameworks and capturing decision
   rationale. So Discovery isn't pre-work before the "real" build; it *is* the foundational asset
   that unlocks every higher level.
2. **Skills and Knowledge compound; Process is what you reconfigure.** A framework-application skill
   built for Reason is reused unchanged in Orchestrate; an encoded operator is an asset that pays
   off at every level above it. Process, by contrast, is re-wired per workflow. So the investment
   logic is: build durable Skills + Knowledge once, recompose Process per workflow — which is
   precisely why the same engine can later extend from strategy into marketing and creative.
3. **The lower rungs are the commitment; the higher rungs are the trajectory.** This engagement
   delivers Recall → Assist → early Reason on a solid foundation. Critique, Orchestrate, and Operate
   are deliberately left as future horizons — real, reachable, and unlocked by the foundation, but
   scoped and priced only once their value is proven. That keeps the promise inside what Discovery
   and 16 weeks can actually support.
