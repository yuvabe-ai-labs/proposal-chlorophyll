# How the Strategy Brain Gets Built — Level by Level

**Document purpose:** Show *how* a chlorophyll-owned strategy intelligence system actually gets
built — as a sequenced climb, not a single leap — and why the order matters. This is a companion to
the Strategy Intelligence Roadmap: the roadmap says *what* each phase delivers; this note explains
*how* it's constructed and where the durable value sits.

> **Read this as an indicative shape, not a fixed plan.** We haven't run Discovery yet, so we don't
> yet know the exact shape of chlorophyll's data, methods, and decision points. Phase 1 (Discovery)
> is precisely what turns this high-level climb into a concrete, scoped build — it confirms or
> reshapes everything in Phases 2–5, and especially how the **Knowledge** layer is structured. The
> agentic spectrum and the three building blocks below are the dependable framework; the specifics
> listed under each later phase are illustrative directions Discovery will validate, refine, or
> replace.

---

## The two ideas this rests on

### 1. The agentic spectrum — a ladder, not a switch

AI doesn't arrive as one capability. It climbs a ladder of autonomy, and each rung is a different
kind of usefulness:

| Rung | What it does | Example for strategy |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Recall** | Finds the relevant past work | "Surface the closest past cases for this brief" |
| **Assist** | Drafts a first cut | "Draft a first territory in our voice" |
| **Reason** | Runs a multi-step chain | "Brief → category map → tested options" |
| **Critique** | Challenges and stress-tests | "Where does this positioning break?" |
| **Orchestrate** | Coordinates many specialist agents | "Run a synthetic strategy workshop" |
| **Operate** | Runs the workflow end-to-end, human-gated | "Brief-to-territory, you approve at the gates" |

The system is built to climb this ladder one secure rung at a time — never running ahead of what
chlorophyll trusts.

### 2. The three building blocks

Every rung is built from the same three things:

- **Knowledge** — what the system *knows*: structured cases, frameworks, decision rationale, market
  context. The substrate.
- **Skills** — what the system can *do*: retrieve, draft, apply a framework, critique, orchestrate.
  Reusable capabilities.
- **Process** — how the work *flows*: the sequence, the checkpoints, and — most importantly — where
  a strategist stays in control.

As the system climbs, **Knowledge** shifts from *documents* to *encoded method* to *captured
judgment*; **Skills** shift from *retrieval* to *reasoning* to *orchestration*; and **Process**
shifts the strategist's role from *operator* to *editor* to *director* to *approver* — always
accountable for the final output.

## The build, phase by phase

*Phase 1 is firm. Phases 2–5 are illustrative — their exact shape, and especially the structure of
the data, is set by what Discovery reveals.*

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Audit · *2–3 weeks*
**Climbs to:** the foundation beneath everything *(pre-Recall)*

- **Knowledge (the whole focus):** inventory and structure the case library; build a shared
  brand-strategy vocabulary; map where anthrop™️, wholon™️, ideantity™️, litmosi™️ and the two
  internal algorithms enter the work; **capture how strategists *decide*, not just what they ship**;
  classify what is reusable versus confidential.
- **Skills:** none built yet — we *specify* which capabilities each later rung will need.
- **Process / your control:** map the real workflow and mark where human judgment must remain
  non-negotiable.

**What you get:** a clear map of your own knowledge, and a scoped, de-risked direction.
**Why it matters:** *this is the asset.* We're structuring your thinking, not buying a generic
tool — and everything above is impossible without it.

### Phase 2 — Focused Strategy Brain Prototype · *2–3 weeks*
**Climbs to:** **Recall → Assist**

- **Knowledge:** structure a focused slice — a handful of cases plus one output type (say, brand
  territory) — with gold-standard exemplars and your voice guide.
- **Skills:** retrieval, similarity matching and summarization *(Recall)*; first-cut drafting in
  your idiom and brief comprehension *(Assist)*.
- **Process / your control:** query → results; draft → **strategist edits** (your edits teach the
  system).

**What you get:** *"Give it a brief — it surfaces the closest past work and drafts a first-cut
territory in your voice. You edit."*
**Why it matters:** tangible proof on *your* material before any deeper spend; it validates trust
and adoption early.

### Phase 3 — Internal Strategy Brain · *3–4 weeks*
**Climbs to:** **Reason**

- **Knowledge:** **encode the IPs as callable reasoning operators** ("run wholon™️ synthesis on
  these inputs"); formalize the two algorithms; capture heuristics for strong versus weak; build a
  pattern library.
- **Skills:** multi-step chaining; framework application; divergent option generation; convergent
  synthesis.
- **Process / your control:** an orchestrated chain with **mid-chain checkpoints** you can inspect
  and redirect, plus a full reasoning trace.

**What you get:** brief → category map → territory options → pressure-tested option set, as a
reviewable chain.
**Why it matters:** this is where your *thinking* becomes software — the moat. Your IP is now
executable, not just searchable.

### Phase 4 — External Research Layer · *3–4 weeks*
**Climbs to:** **Critique**

- **Knowledge:** market, category, competitor and cultural data; **quality signals** (what
  derivative or weak looks like); a risk/objection library; captured *rejection* rationale.
- **Skills:** adversarial red-teaming; rubric-based evaluation; consistency checking (does core →
  territory → naming hold together?); confidence calibration.
- **Process / your control:** a generate → critique → revise loop where the agent challenges and
  **the strategist adjudicates**.

**What you get:** a strategy *sparring partner* that stress-tests positioning against market reality
and your own bar.
**Why it matters:** the senior-strategist challenge function, institutionalized — and only credible
now, because Phase 3 gave it something real to challenge.

### Phase 5 — Integration · *2 weeks*
**Climbs to:** **Orchestrate → Operate**

- **Knowledge:** role and persona definitions (your strategist archetypes); workshop-flow templates;
  governance policy as data; live ingestion of new cases and signals.
- **Skills:** multi-agent orchestration; role specialization; debate and arbitration;
  quality-gating.
- **Process / your control:** diverge → debate → converge, with you as **facilitator at the
  gates**, and governance enforced *in* the flow.

**What you get:** an integrated, governed strategy workflow — a synthetic workshop that runs from
brief toward directions, with strategists directing and approving.
**Why it matters:** the full system — internal knowledge plus external context, composed into one
workflow. And the same engine now extends to marketing and creative.

## Why the sequence works

1. **Discovery is the asset, not the overhead.** The Knowledge built in Phase 1 — your structured
   cases and *encoded method* — is what makes Reason, Critique, and Orchestrate possible. Skip it
   and you get a generic tool; invest in it and you compound an asset.
2. **It compounds.** Skills and Knowledge built early are *reused*, not rebuilt — the wholon™️
   operator from Phase 3 pays off again in Phase 5. Later phases mostly re-wire Process.
3. **You're always in control, and the role grows.** Operator → editor → director → approver.
   Strategist judgment stays accountable at every gate.
